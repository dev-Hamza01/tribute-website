Bhagat Singh

1907-1931

The Flame of Indian Revolution

Bhagat Singh was Born on 28 September 1907 in Banga, Punjab (now in Pakistan), Bhagat Singh was raised in a family deeply involved in the Indian independence movement. Influenced by stories of bravery and patriotism from an early age, he was drawn toward revolutionary ideas and the vision of a free India. His mind matured quickly, and by his teens, he was already immersed in nationalist activities, challenging colonial rule with his sharp intellect and pen.

Biographies

  • Born on 28 September 1907 in Banga, Punjab.
  • Influenced by Jallianwala Bagh massacre and Kartar Singh Sarabha.
  • Joined Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA).
  • Involved in the assassination of John Saunders in 1928.
  • Threw non-lethal bombs in Central Legislative Assembly in 1929.
  • Used trial and jail to promote revolutionary ideas.
  • Hanged on 23 March 1931 in Lahore Jail with Rajguru and Sukhdev.
  • Became a symbol of patriotism, courage, and sacrifice.
